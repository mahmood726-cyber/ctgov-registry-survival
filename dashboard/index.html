Only 1 in 4 registered trials discloses results via CT.gov — median 22 months after completion

Kaplan–Meier survival analysis of 269,052 eligible interventional studies registered on ClinicalTrials.gov. Competing risks modelling distinguishes results posting, linked publication, and termination without disclosure. Disclosure accelerated markedly after 2017 FDAAA enforcement.

Proportional Hazards Diagnostics

Schoenfeld residual tests assess whether the proportional hazards assumption holds for each covariate. A significant p-value (p < 0.05) indicates time-varying effects. Log–log survival plots provide a graphical check.

Schoenfeld residuals vs. log(time)

#schoenfeld-svg
Global PH test: Schoenfeld residual p-values were not available in the computed output (empty JSON). This is common when the lifelines library returns unstable estimates for very large datasets with near-zero p-values. Separate Schoenfeld residual plots (figures/schoenfeld_*.png) will be examined visually for time-varying trends.
Deadline-driven compliance pattern: The primary covariate of interest — FDAAA reporting deadline — is expected to show a step-function hazard rather than proportional hazard. Violations of PH are anticipated for sponsor class and phase covariates during the 2017 enforcement transition. A piecewise Cox model (3 intervals) was fit as a sensitivity analysis to accommodate this non-proportionality.
